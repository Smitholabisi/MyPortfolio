This project is Stockholm Cholera Outbreak of August 1853 was a historical event during which cholera, a waterborne disease, spread throughout the city. Cholera is caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae and leads to severe diarrhea and dehydration. This outbreak was a significant public health concern because it led to the death of about 3,000 people.

The Dataset used in this Analysis may be limited in several ways because it was collected by writing in a church environment. This Dataset was recorded and received in Swedish language. I had to translate it to English using the Google Translate tool.

Insights and findings:
It can be deduced that the total number of deaths during the Stockholm cholera outbreak in 1853 was 248 including male 118 and female 130 while the maximum aged person(s) died was 86. Tuesday recorded the highest amount of deaths, 46 followed by Thursday, 44.

The month with the highest death is september at 183 while the least month of death is November 11. In the same vein, Female had a higher death row with 52.42% compared to men having 47.58%. Also, Mamsell was the top professions affected by death.
